Alexis Adelin Beka Beka, born on March 29, 2001, to his father Verlin Richard Beka Beka, is a proficient French footballer renowned for his role as a defensive midfielder, currently plying his trade with the esteemed Ligue 1 club, Nice.

Born in France, Beka Beka holds dual nationality, proudly claiming both French and Congolese citizenship.

Hailing from Paris, Alexis honed his football skills through a journey that included stints at the Versona and Caen academies, with the latter being the platform where he penned his maiden professional contract.

His foray into senior football commenced in December 2019, when he debuted in a fixture against Clermont in the French Ligue 2.

Throughout his tenure with Caen, Beka Beka amassed 33 appearances and contributed with a solitary goal.

Prior to his ascent in professional football, the talented 20-year-old had earned call-ups to various French youth teams, and most recently, he has been donning the colors of the Olympic team, where he notably participated in three matches during the Tokyo Games.

Alexis Beka Beka, whose father had a background in football, initiated his football journey by obtaining his first license in Verson.

Born in Paris, he moved to the Caen region at 4. In his own words, he recalls, “My grandfather and my father enrolled me at AS Verson.”

His time there, however, spanned only one season, specifically in 2007-2008.

Looking back on those early days, Fabrice Girard, who was his mentor then, affirms, “He may have been outclassed, but his talent was unmistakable.”

Even from that young age, it was evident that Alexis possessed a unique flair for the game.

He had an innate ability to read the field and control the ball with remarkable finesse. Whenever he had possession, it seemed like a whirlwind had been unleashed.

His confidence, passion for the game, and technical prowess were already quite remarkable traits.

His Grandfather Is His Inspiration

Alexis Beka Beka holds a deep-seated affection for Evreux, a city that holds a special place in his heart due to its significance to his father’s side of the family, including his father himself.

“Since I was a child, I’ve frequently visited to see my cousins, uncle, and aunt,” the young man fondly recalls.

This cherished habit remained a constant in his life as he grew older.

He shares, “I still visit almost every weekend after our matches. Many of my friends reside there. In Evreux, I find a tremendous amount of support. It’s where I derive my strength.”

Although he never played for the local club, EFC 27, Alexis Beka Beka maintains strong connections to it.

“I know nearly everyone who plays there,” he affirms.

Moreover, at the outset of the season, during an N3 match against AG Caen, he was spotted in the Venoix aisles, passionately supporting his friends.

His grandfather played a pivotal role in his football journey, faithfully driving him to all his training sessions during his days at the training center, spanning many years.

“He also attends all my matches,” he proudly mentions. Regrettably, the ongoing health crisis has momentarily disrupted this cherished ritual.
