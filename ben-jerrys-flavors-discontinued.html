Four Ben &amp; Jerrys flavors youll never see again and fans are wishing they would come bac

ICE cream lovers have mourned the loss of their favorites after Ben & Jerry’s axed their choice pints.

Many times, big brands discontinue products that have either low sales or cost too much to produce.

Regardless, fans are left disappointed.

Below, we’ve rounded up four ice cream flavors that are still missed by many.

For those missing their favorites, each of these may be found in its "flavor graveyard" where Ben & Jerry's lay them to rest.

"We can mourn their chunks and swirls and keep their sweet memories alive," the ice cream giant said on its website.

1. CRÈME BRULEE

Back in 2007, this ice cream flavor was introduced to the masses quickly becoming a favorite for many.

Crème Brulee was a sweet custard ice cream with a caramelized sugar swirl.

Despite being discontinued in 2012, fans are reeling from the loss.

One ice cream eater wrote on Twitter: “My all time Ben and Jerry's favorite was Creme Brulee. They discontinued it several years ago, I keep wishing they will bring it back.”

2. WAVY GRAVY

The Wavy Gravy flavor was introduced way back in 1993 and lasted until its demise in 2001.

Each pint came packed with caramel and cashew brazil nut ice cream and full of a chocolate hazelnut fudge swirl and roasted almonds.

The company teased on its "gravestone" that it may eventually return.

"Just so there's no confusion. We thought we oughtta warn ya: Wavy Gravy isn't dead - He lives in California," the stone said.

"No such luck for Wavy's flavor, but we've been wrong before, we won't give up the ghost if you won't. So what're you waiting for?"

3. SCHWEDDY BALLS

This one was born out of the classic SNL sketch with cast members Ana Gasteyer, Molly Shannon, and Alec Baldwin.

The sketch was where the actors pretended to be "NPR's Delicious Dish" discussing Alex Baldwin's character's holiday dessert.

Schweddy Balls featured vanilla ice cream with rum, fudge-covered rum, and malt balls.

The flavor was part of its 2011 Holiday Season Limited Batch.

4. FOSSIL FUEL

From 2005 to 2010 the Fossil Fuel flavor was a favorite for many fans.

It was made up of sweet cream ice cream and had chocolate cookie pieces, fudge dinosaurs, and a fudge swirl.

For the anthropology enthusiasts, they got to "dig" for their fudgy dinosaurs while enjoying the sweet cream.

Its gravestone read: "Fudgy dinosaurs galore. Got all dug up til there were no more. A flavor re-design might be do-able. But Fossil Fuel is non-renewable."
