Professional Career

Mowins started her career in Homer, New York, in 1991, as a news and sports editor for WXHC-FM Radio. Later, she moved to ESPN in 1994 and became part of the Greater Syracuse Sports Hall of Fame in 2009. 

She served as the play-by-play announcer on the late coverage of ESPN’s Monday Night Football opening week for the Denver Broncos and Los Angeles Chargers in 2017. 

Beth made history by being the first woman to do play-by-play for nationally televised NFL games. Moreover, she became the first woman to call play-by-play for an NBA game on network TV in 2021. 

The American play-by-play was chosen as the announcer on ESPN’s Monday Night Football between Los Angeles Chargers and Denver Broncos.

That made her the second female play-by-play announcer in NFL regular season. 

She became the first female play-by-play announcer to call college basketball, NBA, and NFL for CBS sports in its 58-year history. 

On May 8, 2021, she became the first woman to call it one of the team’s regular season games. Moreover, Beth made her NBC Olympics debut, hosting the 2020 Tokyo Olympics. 

Mowins serves as a sports journalist and play-by-play for ESPN, CBS, and Marquee Sports Network in 2023.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Did Beth Mowins play sports?

Beth Mowins played basketball, softball, and soccer at North Syracuse High School in North Syracuse, New York.

Who is the female college football announcer?

Beth Mowins is the female college football announcer. In 2017, Beth became the first woman in 30 years to call a National Football League game.  She has called a variety of sports for ESPN, and her primary assignments are announcing men's and women's college sports.

What game is Beth Mowins calling?

Mowins currently serves as the play-by-play announcer and sports journalist for ESPN, CBS, and Marquee Sports Network and calls NBA and NFL games.

Do the Cubs have a female announcer?

Beth Mowins served as the female announcer for the Cubs in 2021. She worked alongside analysts Jim Deshaies and Jon Boog Sciambi. She is the first woman in Cubs history to call a regular-season game.
