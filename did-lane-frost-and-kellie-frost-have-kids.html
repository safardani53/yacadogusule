Did Lane Frost and Kellie Frost Have Kids?

Are you guys great fans of rodeo shows? Does the perfect cowboy costume get your attention every time?

If yes, you might have heard about the former rodeo star, Lane Frost. Lane Frost is a prime example of how rodeo shows can be entertaining for spectators but not for their family members.

Because a rodeo can cost a valuable human life, as it did for Lane Frost.

Yes, the rodeo champion is no more in this world, and the bull took over his life; in Lane Frost’s life, that bull is his grim reaper.

His death shocked everyone, but it devastated the life of one person: his wife, Kellie Frost.

What happened to Kellie Frost after the death of Lane? Please read the entire article to learn about the true events.

Who is Lane Frost?

Lane Frost is a late American rodeo cowboy who competed in many professional rodeo cowboy association events and won the world champion bull rider title in 1987 by taming the bull.

And he is also a Pro Rodeo Hall of Fame inductee. Thus, Lane Frost was a very great rodeo performer during that period.

But unfortunately, in 1989, in the Cheyenne Frontier Days Rodeo competition, the bull named Takin’ Care of Business took the life of the great rodeo star Lane Frost.

Thus, Lane Frost died at the age of 25.

Cause Of Death of Lane Frost

Lane Frost’s death was a great shock for the whole world, but unfortunately, he died during the Cheyenne Frontier Days Rodeo event on July 30, 1989.

During that show, Lane Frost completed his 85-point ride on the bull named “Taking Care of Business,” a Brahma bull. The Brahma bull is a naturally bulkier zebrine-taurine hybrid beef cattle.

After completing his round, suddenly, lane frost fell into the mud, and the Brahma bull hit his back with its sharp horns. It leads to damaged ribs for lane frost.

But he tried to ask for help by walking some small steps, but unfortunately, his heart and lungs got punctured by his broken ribs.

Lane Frost was immediately sent to the local hospital, but the doctors couldn’t help him, and he died.

Where was Lane Frost buried?

Freckles Brown, the great and iconic rodeo star, was the mentor and hero for Lane Frost; thus, Lane’s family and friends decided to bury him near the Freckles Brown Cemetery, which is located in Mount Olivet Cemetery in Hugo, Oklahoma.

Who is Lane Frost Wife?

Lane Frost was married when he died, and his wife’s name was Kellie Frost. Kellie was his high school love, and she was a barrel racer.

Barrel racing is a rodeo event in which the rider and horse must clear the hurdles in the shortest amount of time.

Kellie, who has participated in rodeo events, understands the difficulty involved, so she backed Lane.

On January 5, 1985, the couple married.

Did Lane Frost and Kellie Frost have kids?

No, Lane Frost and Kellie don’t have kids. After five years of marriage, Lane Frost died.

And Lane didn’t have any kids as, well.

Does Lane Frost wife remarry?

Lane Frost died in 1989, and his death significantly impacted Kellie’s life. It took her many years to recover from Lane’s death.

And in 1993, she met another lover, Mike Macy, a great rodeo star. He has appeared in the National Finals Rodeo in Las Vegas.

Does Lane Frost wife have kids?

Yes, Kellie has two children, who were born to her second husband, Mike Macy.

She has one son and one daughter; their son Aaron is 28, and their daughter Brogan is 25.

Aaron is a professional rodeo cowboy like his father, Mike, and Brogan is a barrel racer like her mother, Kellie.

Bottom Line

Lane Frost may not be present with us, but his talent and rodeo shows will always be remembered, and even a film named “8 Seconds” was released in 1993 in memory of Lane Frost.

During that interview for the movie promotion, Kellie shared this message with a worldwide audience.

My husband has been an incredible source of support throughout this entire ordeal. It’s been hard, but to sit there and watch it brings great joy. But we chose to do it, and I think it was a great tribute to Lane. “That’s the most important thing for me.”
