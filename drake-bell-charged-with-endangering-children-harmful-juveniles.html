Drake &amp; Josh star Drake Bell 'charged with endangering children' &amp; 'disseminating matter harmful to

DRAKE Bell has been charged with "attempting to endanger children" though he has pleaded "not guilty."

The Nickelodeon star was previously accused of "verbal and physical abuse" by his ex-girlfriend, though he also denied those claims.

Drake, 34, has been charged with "disseminating matter harmful to juveniles," according to TMZ.

The child star was also accused of "attempted endangering children" but has denied it.

Police arrested the singer in Cleveland, Ohio, and he appeared in Cuyahoga County court on Thursday.

Drake entered a not guilty plea and posted a $2,500 bond which ordered him to have no contact with his alleged victim.

The incident allegedly occurred on December 1st, 2017, the day that the TV star was set to perform at a venue in Cleveland.

The California native was indicted last month and will return to court on June 23rd.

This was not the first time Drake has encountered legal issues, as in August of 2020 he was accused of "verbal and physical" abuse by his ex-girlfriend Melissa Lingafelt.

In a series of TikTok videos, the blonde claimed her famous ex was aggressive towards her when they lived together at ages 16 and 20.

She alleged: "It wasn’t until about a year when the verbal abuse started.

"And when I say verbal abuse, imagine the worst type of verbal abuse you could ever imagine, and that was what I got.

"It then turned into physical – hitting, throwing, everything. At the pinnacle of it, he [dragged] me down the stairs of our house... My face hit every step on the way down. I have photos of this."

Melissa added that she didn't "even want to get into the underage girls thing" but said, "I mean, I will, but I'm scared."

Drake's ex-lover also shared what seemed to be messages from another previous girlfriend, Paydin Layne LoPachin.

The couple dated from 2011 to 2016, and she also claimed to experience a similar situation.

In regards to the various allegations, the iCarly alum responded that he "never abused my ex-girlfriend or did so many of the other things Melissa falsely claimed on her TikTok video."

Drake added that they dated "more than a decade ago," and they "unfortunately, both called each other terrible names, as often happens when couples are breaking up. But that is it.

He slammed: "I do not know if today’s behavior is some kind of misguided quest for more money or attention.

"But I cannot and will not allow these offensive and defamatory allegations to go unchallenged and I am reviewing my legal options."

Drake also claimed that Melissa had reached out for "financial support during a tough time" just a year before coming forward, to which he obliged.
