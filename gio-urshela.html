Gio Urshela | Baseball Career and Highlights

As we have talked about, Gio has always been interested in baseball since a very young age. Likewise, the talented player did not pull any stops while pursuing his career in baseball.

At the early young age of 17, Urshela signed with the Cleveland Indians as an international free agent and made his debut, playing for Dominican Summer League Indians and the Arizona League Indians. 

In 2015, the young player was promoted to the Columbus Clippers and was named the Indians’ fourth-best prospect by MLB.com. With his progress and performances, Urshela was called up to the major leagues on June 8, 2015.

After much waiting, Gio finally made his MLB debut with Cleveland in 2015 and even got his first major league hit, a single, and home run in the same game against the Seattle Mariners.

Later, Urshela played 81 games for Cleveland, hitting .225 with six homers and 21 RBIs. On May 9, 2018, Cleveland traded Urshela to the Toronto Blue Jays for cash. 

But he didn’t last long with the Blue Jays. Just three months after being traded to Toronto, he was again shipped to the New York Yankees, again for cash. 

In April 2019, Gio was selected by the Yankees and was assigned to play in the place of the third baseman Miguel Andujar who was on an injury reserve.

Urshela then debuted as the Yankees starting 3rd baseman in 2020 and played in 43 teams.
