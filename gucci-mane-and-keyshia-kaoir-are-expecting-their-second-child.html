Gucci Mane’s wife’s age, occupation, and other details

Keyshia Ka’Oir, a prominent model, actress, and entrepreneur, was born on January 10, 1985. Her siblings include three boys, and when she was 17, her family relocated to Florida, USA. She began her career as a celebrity stylist before venturing into modeling after getting cast as Timbaland and Drake’s mini-movie and music video Say Something’s jealous ex-wife Lisa. She went on to star in music videos and magazine covers, and she was named XXL Magazine Model of the Year in 2010. Keyshia pursued her dream of becoming an entrepreneur, releasing a slew of bright-colored lipsticks with her cosmetic firm, Ka’oir Cosmetics. The brand has garnered popularity for its cosmetics hues, and many celebrities use it.

Ka’oir glitzsticks, nail lacquers, lip gloss, and other beauty accessories were also introduced. Trina, Snooki, and Teyana Taylor have all inked endorsement agreements with her organization. Furthermore, she has often expressed her support for increased breast cancer awareness. Despite being a well-known figure, she lacks a Wikipedia page, making extensive information on her inaccessible.

Gucci Mane and Keyshia Ka’Oir’s relationship

Keyshia Ka’Oir and Gucci Mane initially met after Mane saw her on magazine covers. She appeared in one of Mane’s music videos, and they became romantically involved shortly after. Mane was subsequently detained on suspicion of illegally possessing guns. He was freed in May 2016 and proposed to Keyshia that November during an Atlanta Hawks game. They married in October 2017, and celebrities such as Sean “Diddy” Combs, Lil Yachty, 2 Chainz, and others were in attendance.

Ice Davis, their first child, was born in December 2020. In December of last year, the couple celebrated his first birthday. They also uploaded photos and videos of Davis beaming while surrounded by opulent Cocomelon-themed décor, customized coloring books, adorned cookies and chocolates, and a three-tier cake. Gucci Mane is a well-known rapper and record producer. In 2005, he released Trap House, which was followed by two further albums, Hard to Kill and Back to the Trap House, in 2006 and 2007.
