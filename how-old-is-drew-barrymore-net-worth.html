How old is Drew Barrymore and whats her net worth?

ACTRESS, Drew Barrymore, is no stranger to the big screen - having starred in a string of successful films since she was a child.

In May 2023 it was revealed that the 48-year-old had dropped out of hosting the MTV Movie & TV Awards in support of the striking members of the Writer's Guild of America.

How old is Drew Barrymore?

Drew Barrymore was born in Culver City, California, on February 22, 1975.

She is an actress, producer, and host of her own CBS daytime talk show, The Drew Barrymore Show.

Barrymore was an acclaimed child actress before she was a teenager - having been born into an acting family, with her father having been the American actor, John Drew Barrymore.

Sadly, Barrymore struggled with demons at a young age.

She was placed in rehab at 13. At 14, she was emancipated from her parents and attempted to commit suicide.

Barrymore spent months in an institution as a teenager, but despite her challenges, she continued acting.

What is Drew Barrymore’s net worth?

As you might imagine, with a career like Barrymore’s, her fortune has grown tremendously over the years.

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Barrymore has a net worth of nearly $125million.

Barrymore has acted in films such as E.T., Poison Ivy, Batman Forever, Scream, The Wedding Singer, Ever After, Never Been Kissed, Charlie’s Angels, 50 First Dates, Music and Lyrics, He’s Just Not That Into You, and Whip It.

She reportedly made $14million for Charlie’s Angels: Full Throttle, the 2003 sequel to the 2000 reboot of the franchise.

Barrymore has also made television appearances, both in person and as a voice. Notably, Barrymore was part of the main cast of Santa Clarita Diet.

She must make a decent paycheck from her talk show because, in March 2021, Barrymore announced she was taking an indefinite hiatus from acting - citing spending time with her kids, Olive and Frankie, as the main reason.

Why did Drew Barrymore drop out of hosting MTV Movie & TV Awards?

On May 4, 2023, it was revealed that Barrymore had stepped down as host of this year’s MTV Movie & TV Awards, as per Variety.

“I have listened to the writers, and in order to truly respect them, I will pivot from hosting the MTV Movie & TV Awards live in solidarity with the strike,” Barrymore said in a statement.

“Everything we celebrate and honor about movies and television is born out of their creation. And until a solution is reached, I am choosing to wait but I’ll be watching from home and hope you will join me. I thank MTV, who has truly been some of the best partners I have ever worked with.

"And I can’t wait to be a part of this next year, when I can truly celebrate everything that MTV has created, which is a show that allows fans to choose who the awards go to and is truly inclusive.​”
