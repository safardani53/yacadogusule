I’M A Celeb fans have moaned 'it’s a fix’ after spotting a ‘clue’ after Nella Rose was ruled out tonight's trial.

This evening it is Jamie Lynn Spears who will take on Climb of Cruelty.

Ant and Dec arrived in camp to share the news with the celebrities at the end of last night's I'm A Celebrity instalment.

Firstly, they told the camp that Nigel Farage and Nella are exempt, leaving the YouTuber very shocked.

Many took to X to point out her reaction which came after her clash with First Dates star Fred Siriex.

One wrote: "Nella looked surprised being ruled out on medical grounds. Bad fix. #ImACeleb."

"The way they were shocked about "medical grounds" it was deffo a set up so that someone else does a trail," a sixth pointed out.

Last night saw Nella erupt at Fred as she demanded him that he stays away from her for the rest of the show.

At the beginning of the episode, Fred offered Nella up some food, but she kept ignoring him.

In the Bush Telegraph, Nella told the camera: "Last night after dinner Fred made a comment about him basically how he could be my dad.

"I took offence to that because one of the first conversations we had together was me opening up about the fact that my dad’s passed away."

"I didn’t say anything about it last night because I was kind of just annoyed about it."

She added: "So I slept on it and I kind of thought about it and I was just like, this is not the kind of person I want to be around."

After trying to catch Nella's attention, Fred walked over to her and she finally told him how she was feeling.

Nella explained: "So yesterday, when I wanted to burn fat that you were going to throw away anyways, it’s not even something that we needed to survive in camp.

"You turned around to me, with an attitude and said, ‘You know I could be your dad, right?’

"To me, I don’t care how you said it to me, it’s disrespectful and I don’t want to talk to you.

"I don’t want to be around you. I only allow people to disrespect me once. You hurt my feelings. I was very upset… you keep trying to speak to me when I don’t want to speak to you.

She added: "I would rather stay away from you, I don’t want to eat your food, I don’t want to talk to you. We can just live, you live on that side, I live on this side."

Fred apologised for what he had said, but meant no offence.

He replied: "I am so sorry. Can I just say? First of all, I am sorry I’ve offended you. I am unaware that you felt offended.

"I am unaware that what I said would lead to this conversation, I only said that in a way because I am older than you, I am 51 and you’re 26."
