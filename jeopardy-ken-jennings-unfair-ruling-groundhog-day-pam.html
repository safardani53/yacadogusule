Jeopardy! fans spot host Ken Jennings 'hesitating' before 'questionable ruling'

JEOPARDY! fans have spotted host Ken Jennings hesitating before accepting what was called an incorrect response during Wednesday’s episode.

Even the contestant who answered it revealed she thought the ruling was questionable.

Pam - a New York-based dentist and entrepreneur - faced Renee Russell - a Baltimore-based branch office administrator - and James Fraser - a California-based naval aviator who won the Oct. 19th episode wearing his uniform.

The clue for $200 under "Holidays & Observances" read: "Punxatawney, Pennsylvania celebrates this on February 2, year after year after year."

Pam buzzed in and responded: "What is 'Groundhog's Day?"

Ken, 48, replied: "You -" then taking an audible two-second pause off-camera, added: "Got it."

'GROUNDHOG'S DAY?'

Pam's score increased to $4,400 and the match swiftly moved on, though viewers at home didn't do so.

Many felt the judges should have not accepted her response as the name of the holiday is 'Groundhog Day' with no 'S', per the Library of Congress.

One Twitter user asked: "Was that long 'youuuuuu' glancing at the judges to see if 'Groundhog's' was okay?"

A second agreed: "Had to be!" agreeing Ken had probably looked towards the judges while hesitating.

A third Twitter user also claimed: "I watched this in the studio and I was pretty surprised that it didn't get any pushback.

I don't think I've ever heard an 's' added to the holiday name before."

Yet another fan who claimed to have attended the taping wrote to Reddit: " I was pretty surprised that Pam saying "Groundhog's Day" didn't get any pushback in the studio, especially with Ken's hesitance. 

"I was expecting a score reversal."

'I KNEW IT WAS MAYBE A PROBLEM'

Pam, who had been deemed correct, replied and said she agreed that in the name of fairness, she thought it should have been reversed.

"I assumed [that's why Ken hesitated]. I knew it was maybe a problem as soon as it exited my mouth," she tweeted in reply to the first user.

KEN-TROVERSIAL?

During Ken's his first official week on the job in the now-airing Season 39 the 74-time G.O.A.T. scored enormous backlash.

As outlets like CNN reported after it was covered by The U.S. Sun upon airing, Ken "allowed a contestant to change his answer."

The champion–turned–host seemed to breeze by a now-controversial self-correction by reigning winner Luigi de Guzman.

Contestant Luigi buzzed in under the "Cons" category - A painting was shown on the screen as Ken read: "Here's a typical 19th-century landscape by this British painter."

"Who is Constant?" Luigi answered.

Ken then prompted him to repeat himself - the host replied: "Say it again."

"Sorry, who is Constable?" the player then replied, and his answer was accepted in the deeply divisive moment.

It was made worse as later in the same game a female player was cut off from self-correcting by Ken, outlets like People called it a "gender bias."

Luigi posted to Reddit agreeing it was a sticky situation: "The rule went my way, but I can see how it could have easily gone the other way."

Show execs spoke up about the incident and said Ken asked the player to repeat himself because the host didn't hear him, and his self-correction was allowed because Ken hadn't ruled yet.

TOURNAMENT SEASON

Jeopardy!'s never-before-done Second Chance Tournament started airing on October 17th - Jessica Stephens, Molly Karol and James, who beat Pam, have won so far.

The victor of each week’s tournament bracket will then score the two remaining slots in the Tournament of Champions which airs directly afterward.

When both conclude, regular episodes and reigning ace Cris Pannullo will return in a month.
