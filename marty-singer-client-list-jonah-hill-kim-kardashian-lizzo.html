LIZZO'S attorney has defended numerous other celebrities embroiled in legal issues.

Marty Singer, who is newly representing Lizzo as she faces claims of toxicity in her workplace, has worked for clients ranging from Kim Kardashian to Bill Cosby to Jonah Hill and more.

Singer, a famed attorney in California who has received the Entertainment Lawyer of the Year award, has repped Kardashian multiple times and as recently as 2022.

Kardashian called Singer in an episode of The Kardashians on Hulu when her son, Saint, found an ad promising "unreleased footage" of her 17-year-old sex tape.

Singer also worked with Kardashian when it was alleged that she staged her wedding to Kris Humphries in 2012.

The marriage infamously lasted for just 72 days.

In 2021, the attorney sent a cease-and-desist letter to an Instagram model after she claimed that Thompson was sleeping with her while he was dating Khloe.

LIZZO LAWSUIT

The civil lawsuit filed on Tuesday in Los Angeles County Superior Court accuses Lizzo, 35, and her production company Big Grrrl Big Touring, Inc. of creating a hostile work environment.

Arianna Davis, Crystal Williams, and Noelle Rodriguez have made numerous allegations including sexual, religious, and racial harassment, disability discrimination, assault, and false imprisonment.

The lawsuit claims that after a gig in Amsterdam, Lizzo and her crew went to a sexually themed show at a club in the city's notorious Red Light District.

It says Lizzo began inviting cast members to take turns touching the nude performers.

But it is alleged the About Damn Time singer led a chant during the show pressuring Davis to touch the breasts of one of the naked women.

The complaint claims that the chorus became overwhelming and a mortified Davis reluctantly gave in in a bid to end the chants.

Lizzo, who regularly appears to champion body positivity, is also said to have called out Davis for her weight gain and accused of her not being committed to her role.

Meanwhile, Lizzo's dance team captain Shirlene Quigley - who served as a judge on the singer's reality show Lizzo's Watch Out for the Big Grrrls - is accused in the lawsuit of pushing her Christian beliefs onto dancers.

The court filing claims Quigley referred to Davis as a "non-believer" and told co-workers that: "No job and no one will stop me from talking about the Lord."

Davis was fired in May for recording a meeting during which Lizzo had given out notes to dancers about their performances, according to the complaint.

The legal complaint seeks unspecified damages and names Melissa Viviane Jefferson, known professionally as Lizzo, her production company Big Grrrl Big Touring, Inc., and Shirlene Quigley.

'FABRICATED' ALLEGATIONS

Lizzo broke her silence on Thursday morning, days after the lawsuit was filed.

"These last few days have been gut wrenchingly difficult and overwhelmingly disappointing," she wrote.

"My work ethic, morals and respectfulness have been questioned. My character has been criticized.
