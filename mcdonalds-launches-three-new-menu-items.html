McDonald's has launched three new menu items for fall - including glazed donuts

MCDONALD’S has introduced new products for the fall, including glazed donuts available nationwide.

Most of these products being introduced by the fast food giant are dessert items – but know some are only available at select stores.

Glazed Pull Apart Donut

Since September 15, the glazed donut has been included on the McDonald's menu.

However, it will be only included on the menu for a limited amount of time.

According to McDonald’s, the seven-piece pull-apart donut comes with 15 grams of sugars and 310 calories.

Currently, it is the only donut listed on McDonald’s menu.

While prices can vary by state and city, this item sells for $1.59 on the McDonald’s app.

Pumpkin creme pie

Another treat, known as the pumpkin & creme pie, is available at some McDonald’s locations.

Based on the image, you’d expect it to taste similar to McDonald's well-known apple pie, with the only difference being on the inside.

But unfortunately, expect the pumpkin and creme pie to disappear after the fall season.

McDonald’s confirmed to The Sun that this item is available now for a limited amount of time at select restaurants.

The apple pie at McDonald’s normally sells for about $1.49.

Pumpkin spice latte

If you aren’t a proponent of the pumpkin spice latte at Starbucks, then you might want to try one at McDonald’s.

The sugary beverage includes Arabica coffee blended with cinnamon and pumpkin flavors, with a choice of non-fat or whole dairy.

This product is available as iced or hot at select stores for a limited time, according to McDonald’s.

Also, the pumpkin spice latte has been said to cost around $2.

McPlant to launch in the US?

The McPlant burger has a chance to be the future of McDonald’s, with it becoming a healthier alternative when compared to traditional meat.

The plant-based burger, developed in a partnership with Beyond Meat, has been launched in Sweden, Denmark, and Austria.

Moreover, the McPlant is set to launch in the UK in some restaurants by the end of the month.

McDonald’s told Eat This, Not That! earlier this week that that once each market is set for the McPlant test, a decision will be made judiciously.

It said: "We expect other McDonald's markets will test the McPlant later this year and into the future.”

Meanwhile, McDonald’s told us that it does not have an update on the McPlant potentially entering the US market.
