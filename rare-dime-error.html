I'm a coin collector - your dime could be worth $26,000, the exact front error to look for

A TIKTOKER has revealed a rare dime that sold last year for over $26,000 based on one major error.

The dime was misprinted with the same die on the front and back, meaning it has no obverse.

Due to this minting error, the coin has two tails sides, and Roosevelt is nowhere to be found.

The rare coin was shared on TikTok by coin collector Eric Miller.

Eric is the go-to guy when it comes to valuable money.

He’s known as thecoinchannel on TikTok and typically posts videos about coins, their rarities, and the details that make them valuable.

“This is one of the biggest errors from 2021 that nobody is talking about,” Eric said on TikTok.

The dime was certified by Professional Coin Grading Services (PCGS) and sold by Heritage Auctions for $26,400.

More about Roosevelt dimes

The first Roosevelt dime was produced in 1946 and was an instant hit in post-WWII America, according to the coin website Jmbullion.

The dime was created by John Sinnock, the U.S. Mint's chief engraver from 1925 to 1947.

Although, many historians credit sculptor Selma Burke for the design, including Roosevelt's son and the Smithsonian American Art Museum, according to the academic website Gale.

Many of these types of dimes are worth more than their original value due to its high silver content.

Most are somewhere in the range of $1 up to $4 if found in good condition.

It may not seem like much, but due to the ten-cent face value, a $3 price point is not too bad.

How is the value of a coin determined?

The Roosevelt dime had such a high value due to its grade and error.

Errors happen when the mint incorrectly manufactures a coin, leading to elements that are not supposed to be included.

However, experts determine a coin's value based on a variety of factors.

This includes mintage, errors, demand, age, design and condition.

Pennies especially can be valuable because of their rich history.

More importantly, coins are graded on a rigid scale which is what collectors will pay big bucks for.

There are five main components when it comes to a grading scale including strike, surface preservation, luster, coloration and eye appeal.

Coins are assigned a whole number between 1 and 70, with 70 being a flawless coin.

How to find rare coins

Make sure to check your spare change for any rare pennies, nickels, dimes and other coins as many of them could sell for thousands.

Typically, the most valuable coins are ones with a low mintage or an error, as collectors find those coins are some of the rarest.

Additionally, coin experts will pay top dollar if they are also well-struck.

A great website to check if your coins are worth anything is eBay.

You just need to search the full coin name, select the "sold" listing and then toggle the search to "highest value”.
