Most valuable coins featuring US presidents worth up to $264k including Lincoln pennies - see exact

YOU may be surprised what your coins could be worth – including ones that feature common presidents.

Taking a look at the coins today, many are probably familiar with pieces featuring Abraham Lincoln (penny), Thomas Jefferson (nickel), and George Washington (quarter).

While these are common faces, they could sell for thousands – if you happen to find the right one.

Typical reasons why some are worth more than others are low mintages, or errors.

Also, how much coins are worth often depends on grade and condition.

We round up the most valuable coins that feature US Presidents below.

Just keep in mind in mind that some of these coins might be uncirculated, which means they likely won't turn up in your spare change.

Uncirculated coins are graded in between MS-60 and MS-70.

To see if your coins are worth anything, you can check eBay by searching the full name, selecting the "sold" listing, and then toggle the search to "highest value.

1971 Eisenhower silver dollar – $264,000

Those who are familiar with dollar coins might be familiar with the one that features 34th President Dwight D. Eisenhower.

The Eisenhower silver dollar was only struck by the US mint from 1971 to 1978. 

In particular, a 1971 Eisenhower dollar coin with an “S” mark sold for a whopping $264,000 in a recent auction held by Heritage Auctions.

The coin has been described by Heritage Auctions as being the "birth certificate of America's last silver dollar".

“This is an extremely rare discovery of an early design stage for the first year of the Ike dollars that were made from 1971 to 1978," said Jim Halperin, co-chairman of Heritage Auctions.

"There isn’t even an example of this prototype in the National Numismatic Collection at the Smithsonian."

Prototype coins are typically used to help an engraver perfect the design and are produced within the mint.

1992 Lincoln penny - $25,000

There are quite a few Lincoln pennies that could be valuable, including a 1992 coin. 

The most valuable one is the close AM design. 

To be specific, the two letters on the “America” (on the reverse) should be nearly touching.

According to Coinappraiser, a site that claims it has a group of industry professionals, only 10 or so of close AM coins exist.

It added: "Not many have been certified by the leading coin grading services."

As far as value goes, a close AM Lincoln coin sold for more than $25,000 in 2017.

Meanwhile, another close Lincoln cent sold for $244 on eBay.

1955 Lincoln penny- $17,057

One of the most famous error coins in existence is the 1955 Lincoln double die obverse penny.

For those who aren’t familiar with this error, it means that there is a duplicate of letters, numbers, or words on a coin.

For instance, on this coin, you can clearly see “In God We Trust,” “Liberty,” and “1955” all doubled on the obverse of the coin. 

“The 1955 Doubled Dies were created when the Mint struck a working hub and a working die together while they were both slightly rotated differently from one another,” Professional Coin Grading Service’s Jamie Hernandez wrote in a description of the coin. 

This of course created the double die effect – which was mixed with millions of normal strike versions of the coin. 

According to USA Coin Book, the 1955 double die penny is worth $1,318 on average and up to $17,057 if in “uncirculated (MS-63)” condition. 

As far as recent eBay listings go, one sold for $1,825 online, another sold for $1,801, and another went for $1,552.

1932 Washington D quarter - $13,909

As long as there is a D mintmark on a Washington quarter, then you may have one of the most valuable coins in the series. 

In fact, the 1932 S and 1932 D quarters are the only ones in the series that have mintages under 1million.

Just 408,000 and 436,800 have been minted respectively, according to PCGS' David Hall.

“Both coins have been highly sought after in all grades for the past 60 years,” Mr Hall wrote in a description of the coin.

“They are the key dates of the Washington quarter series and actually are two of the better known rare coins of the twentieth century.”

But the Washington D quarter is more valuable in higher grades.

The D quarter could be worth up to $13,909, while the S quarter’s maximum value is $5,544 in “uncirculated (MS-65)” condition. 

1945 Jefferson nickel - $922

In some cases, grade and condition could drive up the value of a coin – which is the case with one Jefferson coin that sold recently. 

For the most part, a high volume of Jefferson coins are minted each year in the series. 

For example, the lowest mintage in the Jefferson series is said to be the 1950-D coin, which exceeds 2.6million pieces. 

In comparison, nearly 59million 1945 Jefferson S coins have been minted.

If you happen to find one in average condition, the coin might only be worth $1.80, according to USA Coin Book.

However, a 1945 Jefferson S nickel in an “MS 67” grade that was certified by the Numismatic Guaranty Company, sold for $922 recently.

In other rare coin news, a counterfeit Jefferson coin recently sold for more than $400 online.
