Roma sack manager Eusebio Di Francesco after crashing out of Champions League - with Ranieri tipped

ROMA have sacked manager Eusebio Di Francesco after they crashed out of Champions League at the hands of Porto - with Claudio Ranieri tipped to take over.

The Serie A giants slumped to last-16 defeat in Europe and also sit a massive 28 points behind Juventus in the league.

Now Roma have announced they are parting ways with Di Francesco, 49, in a statement on their website.

President Jim Pallotta said: "For my part and for the whole of AS Roma, I would like to thank Eusebio for his efforts.

"He has always worked with a professional attitude and has placed the interests of the club above all personal ones.

"We wish him the best for his career."

Di Francesco won the Serie A title with Roma in 2000-01 as a player - and took over as boss in 2017.

However, he has been shown the door in the midst of a very disappointing 2018-19 season.

Ranieri is reportedly on the club's shortlist to return to his home country as manager.

The Italian was given the boot by Fulham last month with the West Londoners in the Premier League relegation zone.
