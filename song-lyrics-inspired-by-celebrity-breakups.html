Song Lyrics Inspired by Celebrity Breakups

From Taylor Swift and Jake Gyllenhaal to Britney Spears and Justin Timberlake, Us Weekly is revisiting the most famous song lyrics inspired by celebrity breakups.

Swift, for her part, has always been open about her writing about her ex-boyfriends. “If guys don’t want me to write bad songs about them, they shouldn’t do bad things,” the Grammy winner infamously quipped in 2009.

The “We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together” singer — whose past high-profile relationships include Joe Jonas, Harry Styles, Calvin Harris, John Mayer, Tom Hiddleston, Joe Alwyn and Matty Healy — seemingly inspired bestie Selena Gomez, who scored her first No. 1 hit with a song inspired by her on-again, off-again romance with Justin Bieber.

“I gave my all and they all know it / Then you tore me down and now it’s showing / In two months, you replaced us / Like it was easy,” Gomez sings in “Lose You to Love Me,” referring to Justin’s marriage to Hailey Bieber.

After the song dropped, a source confirmed to Us that Gomez’s rocky romance with Justin inspired the track.
