Career

He worked as a journalist for the Croydon Advertiser after graduating. Later, he became interested in acting after being inspired by the story of Trevor Eve, and at the age of 25, he enrolled in the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School. As a stage actor, he has played Prior Walter in ‘Angels in America’ in 1993, Hamlet in 1994, Uncle Vanya in 1998, and Macbeth in 2005, among many others. Then, in 1990, Stephen Dillane made his big-screen debut as Horatio in the drama film adaptation of ‘Hamlet.’

In 1997, he was cast in the historical war drama film ‘Welcome to Sarajevo’ as Michael Nicholson, a British journalist. In 2002, he portrayed Leonard Wolf in the American psychological thriller film ‘The Hours.’ In 2008, he played Thomas Jefferson in the miniseries “John Adams.” He was cast as Stannis Baratheon in the HBO series Game of Thrones in 2011. Then he joined the cast of ‘Hunted,’ where he played Rupert Keel. In the same year, he co-starred with Clémence Guichard in the crime series ‘The Tunnel’ as Karl Roebuck.

In 2012, he was cast as wealthy industrialist Harry Papadopoulos in the British film, Papadopoulos & Sons, in which his own Frank Dillane played his son. He appeared in the films Zero Dark Thirty’ and ‘Twenty8K’ the following year. In 2021, he earned the role of Richard in the British romantic comedy Christmas film ‘Boxing Day,’ and he joined the cast of the espionage thriller series ‘Red Election’ as William Ogilvy.
