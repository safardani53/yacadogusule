Stephen Arthur Stills is an American singer, songwriter and multi-instrumentalist best known for his work with Buffalo Springfield and Crosby, Stills, Nash & Young. As a solo actor and member of two successful bands, Stills has combined record sales of more than 35 million albums. He was ranked 28th on Rolling Stone's 2003 list of "100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time" and 47th on the 2011 list. Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. According to Neil Young, “Stephen is a genius”. Beginning his professional career with Buffalo Springfield, he wrote "For What It's Worth" which went on to become one of the most popular songs of the 1960s. Other notable songs he played while contributing to the group are "Sit Down, I Think I Love You "," Bluebird "and" Rock & Roll Woman ". According to band member Richie Furay, he is "the heart and soul of Buffalo Springfield".
