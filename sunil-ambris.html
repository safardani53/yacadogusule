Career

During the 2013-14 Regional Four Day Competition, Ambris made his Windward Islands debut against Guyana. Despite only playing in six games during his debut season, he finished second on the Windwards’ all-time run-scoring chart behind Devon Smith with 468 runs batted in. He scored two centuries as part of the Windward Islands’ win over the Leeward Islands in March 2017. He was the Windward Islands’ leading run-scorer in the 2016-17 Regional Super.

On September 29, against England, Ambris made his ODI debut for the West Indies, scoring 38 points off 27 balls. When the West Indies faced New Zealand in November 2017, Ambris was part of the Test team.

Against New Zealand on December 1, 2017, he made his Test debut for the West Indies, becoming only the sixth batsman in history to have been out-hit wicket from the first delivery and the first on his Test debut.
