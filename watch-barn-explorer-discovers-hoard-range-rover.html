A BARN find expert has discovered a whopping hoard of Range Rovers in a field - and one of them has only chalked up 23,000 miles.

The urban explorer found the hidden gems in a forest in Wales, and the cars appeared to have been abandoned for a number of years.

The car fanatic documented his incredible findings on his YouTube channel @ImStokze - where he showcased the goldmine of old classic cars.

The video starts in a courtyard, and in the distance you can make out a green Subaru Impreza that has fallen into a state of complete disrepair.

Next up, there's a Range Rover covered in moss that has incredibly covered only 23,000 miles - with the last time it hit the road being back in 2005.

The poster said: "Look how clean the interior and the buttons are. This is so sad; this car includes the original stereo tape player.

"Even the heater controls are in pristine condition. Look how clean the lighter is. It hasn't been used, and look how shiny it is."

Then the urban explorer opens the bonnet of the car and discovers that both the engine and the battery appear in surprisingly clean condition despite having been abandoned for a while.

He continues: "Look at the tread of the tyres. They are almost brand new. The car underneath isn't that bad, really. It's not in bad condition, considering where the car is standing."

Later, the poster uncovers a four-door Range Rover in silver that was manufactured in 1987.

Unfortunately, the abandoned motor is locked, and the explorer is unable to check the car's interior.

But, the enthusiast insists that spending some money to restore the 'Rangie' to its former glory would be a worthwhile job.

The video has gone viral on YouTube with more than 14,497 views and hundreds of comments.

And users were blown away after watching the video online.

One said: "Another great video Stu. As much as I don’t share your passion for the land yachts, it’s crazy to see a 23k miles vehicle of any make just abandoned like that.

"They say there’s a story behind everything, and it’d be great to hear why those ended up just left to rot."

Another person commented: "Great video, mate, unbelievable how that range rover is left to rot."
