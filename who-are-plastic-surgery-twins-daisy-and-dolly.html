Who are plastic surgery twins Daisy and Dolly?

TWIN sisters Daisy and Dolly left Holly and Phil shocked on This Morning as they opened up about how much they have spent on surgery in order to look "more identical".

Here’s everything you need to know...

Who are plastic surgery twins Daisy and Dolly?

Dolly and Daisy Simpson are 25-year-old twins from Stockton-on-Tees that appeared on This Morning talking about their surgery.

The twins have admitted to spending hundreds of thousands on surgery to look like each other. 

They revealed that the cause for all the surgery is because they were bullied in school and felt very insecure about their looks.

They worked as strippers to fund their lifestyle before Covid came along.

Left clueless about their future, the pair then decided to make an OnlyFans account calling themselves the "Double D’s" which stands for Daisy and Dolly.

They now make a mouth watering £10k a month from their Only Fans, and use the money to go towards their surgeries.

How much have Daisy and Dolly spent on plastic surgery?

The twins started getting work done from the age of 18.

They have admitted to spending £140k on their surgeries combined in hopes to look “more identical”.

Here's a list of everything they've had done:

  • Lip-filler
  • Botox
  • Nose-jobs
  • Boob-jobs
  • Labiaplasty (procedure done to reduce the size of the flaps of skin on each side of the vagina)

Daisy is still yet to have a labiaplasty due to it being delayed by Covid.

Dolly has also mentioned how she wants to have a Brazilian butt lift, but Daisy was concerned about statistics that the surgery can kill 1 in 3,000 people.

What have Daisy and Dolly said?

The twins have been very open and honest about their surgeries and the reasoning behind it.

Appearing on This Morning, with host Phillip Schofield and Holly Willoughby asking: “So you want to make yourselves look identical but you both have very different faces.”

Daisy replied saying it's about becoming their “ideal selves”, she added: “We do want to look similar, and we want the same surgeons, but she's a little more extreme than me.  I don't want to look exactly the same as her.”

They went on to explain that it was something that stemmed from whilst they were at school as they were bullied for having white hair and people would refer to them as “Albinos”.

Dolly replied: “We have been bullied all our lives but the big surgeries have fixed those insecurities,” 

“It does get addictive because you can completely transform yourself and you think, If I like the results of me doing this, maybe I will like myself even more if I do that.”
