Who was F Murray Abraham's late wife Kate Hannan?

On April 7, 1962, F. Murray Abraham tied the knot with Kate Hannan.

The Oscar winner and Kate first crossed paths when the former moved to Los Angeles to pursue his acting career.

The couple went on to have two kids together; a daughter named Jamili and a son named Mick.

Kate's loved ones remember her as "extraordinarily courageous, fiercely loyal, and always honest."

What was Kate's cause of death?

On November 19, 2022, Kate lost her battle with multiple sclerosis and died peacefully at her New York City home.

She was survived by Murray, their two children, and their granddaughter Hannan.

In the wake of her passing, Murray revealed he found it difficult to take on dramatic roles.

During his interview with NPR, he confessed that Kate's demise led him to take on comical characters such as Bert Di Grasso in season 2 of The White Lotus.

He told the outlet: "Yeah, I'm still trying to adjust to it. There is a play that I was going to do for theater for a new audience called The Chairs and I had been planning to do it for some time. 

"I had to tell my producer friend Jeffrey that I couldn't do the play. I had to cancel because at the end of the play, the husband and wife [die by] suicide.

"I didn't think I could possibly face that play killing myself at the end of each performance.

"So I canceled out of it. It's affected my life. Absolutely. And I've decided the only thing I can do now is to do comedy just to keep myself off the ground."
