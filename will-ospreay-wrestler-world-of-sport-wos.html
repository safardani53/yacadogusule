WORLD OF SPORT Wrestling is back on British TV as professional wrestling returns to a Saturday night prime-time slot. 

The original WOS Wrestling aired between 1965 and 1985. 33 years on, the series returns with a star line-up, including Britain's New Japan-Pro Wrestling competitor Will Ospreay.

Who is Will Ospreay and how old is he?

Will Ospreay is an English professional wrestler. He currently competes in the Japanese promotion, New Japan-Pro Wrestling where he has featured since 2016.

He was born on May 7, 1993 in Havering, London. He is 25 years old.

Ospreay made his professional debut at the BritWres-Fest in April 2012, featuring as a masked character, Dark Britannico.

What titles has Will Ospreay won and where does he compete?

Will Ospreay was announced as a member of the New Japan-Pro Wrestling stable, Chaos, in March 2016.

In June 2016, Ospreay beat Ryusuke Taguchi to win the 2016 Best of the Super Juniors to become the youngest winner in the history of the tournament and the first-ever Brit to win the crown.

He was also first Brit to win the IWGP Junior Heavyweight title in October 2017 defeating Kushida after suffering defeat in all four of his previous fights against the Japanese star.

He lost the title a month later after losing to Marty Scurll but regained it in January 2018. Ospreay lost the title in his fourth defence,  losing to Hiromu Takahashi in June.

What else does Will Ospreay do?

Will Ospreay has started his own promotion in the United Kingdom, Frontline Wrestling.

They ran their first event, "Build me an Empire", in June 2018.

He is in a relationship with the female wrestler and current Defiant Wrestling women's champion Bea Priestley.
