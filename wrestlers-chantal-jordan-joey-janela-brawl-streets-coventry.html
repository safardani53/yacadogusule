Man and girl, 15, brawl on top of cars and phone box in brutal wrestling match on streets of Coventr

A FIGHT between two professional wrestlers spilled out of the ring and onto a bus, restaurant and on top of a parked car in Coventry.

Chantal Jordan, 15, from the West Midlands, and 'The Bad Boy' Joey Janela, a 29-year-old US wrestling star, grappled on the streets in a 'falls count anywhere' bout.

Video footage showed a crowd of wrestling fans cheering on the pair as they fought in the streets on Friday night.

The rules of the 'Kamikaze pro wrestling' match meant they could leave the ring in The Empire and finish wherever they wanted.

They were seen fighting on a National Express Coventry bus and inside a restaurant.

To screams from the excitable crowd, Chantal leapt from a bus stop on to the American wrestler, before he performed his 'piledriver' on the Brit on a parked car.

They then returned to the original venue where they finished the fight and Janela was declared the winner.

Tom Mills, who filmed the encounter, said: "It was absolute carnage and entertaining to see some amazing talent like Chantal and Joey compete in such a crazy match.

"I will never ever forget it."

Speaking after the fight Chantal told Coventry Live: "When the match with Joey Janela was announced at Kamikaze Pro, I was very buzzed, and to be honest, after seeing Joey's work, I was expecting to be doing something crazy.

"Working with Joey Janela was absolutely amazing, and he is so professional, a really safe worker, and I do have a lot of respect for the guy.

"He gave me the best match of my career so far and I really hope I can work with him again in the future.

"The feedback has been overwhelming, everyone I've spoken to has praised the match and said it is one of the best matches they've witnessed live this year."
